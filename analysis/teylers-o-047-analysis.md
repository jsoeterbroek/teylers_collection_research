## Object Description

A large drawing on paper (357 x 478 mm) by **Rembrandt Harmenszoon van Rijn** (Leiden, 1606 – Amsterdam, 1669), inscribed and dated **"Rembrandt f 1634"**, depicting ten disciples arranged in a circle around Christ in the **Garden of Gethsemane** on the night before his arrest (Matthew 26:36–46; Mark 14:32–42; Luke 22:39–46). Christ's illuminated head and gesture suggest he is speaking; two disciples sleep, one yawns prominently. A strip of paper was removed from the right edge "in antiquity," accounting for the absence of the eleventh or twelfth disciple. Executed in pen in brown and black ink, brush in grey and brown wash, black, red and green chalk, and white heightening. Catalogued as **Benesch 89** in the standard catalogue raisonné. One of Rembrandt's largest and most technically elaborate drawings. Acquired by Teylers at auction in **1796 for 60 guilders**. Exhibited at the Louvre's major exhibition *Rembrandt et la figure du Christ* (2011). Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. TITLE STRIPS THE GETHSEMANE LOCATION — MAKES SUBJECT AMBIGUOUS (MAJOR)

The catalog title is **"Christus en de discipelen"** — Christ and the disciples. The museum's own public-facing texts (topstukken page, collection URL slug, and all secondary sources) use the full title **"Christus en de discipelen in het Hof van Gethsemane."** Without the locative phrase, a researcher cannot determine from the title whether this depicts the Last Supper, the Olivet Discourse, a teaching scene, or the Passion — they are entirely different iconographic subjects. The Louvre database (entry r04), Wikidata (Q21598836), and every serious secondary source use the full title. Truncating it is a significant cataloging failure.

### 2. BENESCH 89 ABSENT FROM BIBLIOGRAPHY (MAJOR)

The catalog cites four references but omits the single most important: **Otto Benesch, *The Drawings of Rembrandt*, 2nd enlarged ed., 6 vols. (London/New York: Phaidon, 1973), no. 89**. The Benesch number is the universal scholarly identifier for Rembrandt drawings — cited in every auction catalogue, museum record, and scholarly article on this work. Additionally, the revised online catalogue at **rembrandtcatalogue.net** notes a dating issue under Benesch 89 ("1631 and 1634?"), questioning whether the inscription date truly reflects the drawing's execution. This scholarly debate is entirely unacknowledged in the Teylers record.

### 3. NO PROVENANCE IN DATABASE (MAJOR)

The AdLib record contains no provenance. The museum's own public page documents that the drawing was **acquired at auction in 1796 for 60 guilders** — notably low compared to a Jan van Huysum still life from the same sale that fetched 740 guilders. The auction is not identified. No pre-1796 ownership history is given or noted as unknown. For a drawing acquired in 1796, a 230-year gap in provenance documentation is an exceptional deficiency for a flagship Topstuk.

### 4. NO EXHIBITION HISTORY (MAJOR)

No exhibition history is recorded. The drawing has been in at least two significant exhibitions:
- **Louvre / Philadelphia / Detroit: *Rembrandt et la figure du Christ* / *Rembrandt and the Face of Jesus*** (Louvre, April–July 2011; Philadelphia Museum of Art, August–October 2011; Detroit Institute of Arts, November 2011 – February 2012)
- ***The 100 Most Beautiful Rembrandts***, Teylers Museum, 2013

### 5. NO DESCRIPTION OF THE SCENE (MAJOR)

The database record has no narrative description field. A researcher consulting the catalog learns only the title, date, and medium — not what the scene depicts, how it is composed, what makes it art-historically significant, or why it is a Topstuk. The museum's own website describes the ten disciples in a circle, the illuminated Christ, the sleeping and yawning figures, and the excised strip — none of this appears in the database record.

### 6. OBJECT NAME FIELD MISUSED — RECURRING STRUCTURAL ERROR (MINOR)

The "Object Name" field reads **"pen en penseel in inkt"** — a medium descriptor. This field should contain the object type: **"tekening"** (drawing), which is correctly given separately in the "Object Category" field. This is the same structural data-model error found across multiple Teylers Topstukken entries.

### 7. CONDITION NOTE ABSENT — EXCISED STRIP NOT RECORDED (MINOR)

Secondary sources note that a strip of paper was removed from the right edge of the sheet **"in antiquity"**, explaining the absence of one or two disciples. This is a material physical fact about the object's condition and history — an irreversible alteration that should be documented in a condition or notes field. It is entirely absent from the catalog record.

### 8. DATING UNCERTAINTY NOT FLAGGED (MINOR)

The catalog assigns the date **"1634; gedateerd"** (1634; dated), taking the inscription at face value. The revised Benesch catalogue at **rembrandtcatalogue.net** flags uncertainty: **"1631 (and 1634)?"**, suggesting the inscription date may not reflect the drawing's actual execution date. A research-quality record should note this scholarly debate.

### 9. SUBJECT TERMS DO NOT IDENTIFY THE BIBLICAL EPISODE (MINOR)

The subject terms (Christus, Gethsemane, discipelen) identify the location but not the specific event. Standard iconographic practice requires identifying the episode: **Agony in the Garden** (Christ's prayer at Gethsemane, the canonical Iconclass subject). No Iconclass code is assigned, which limits discoverability through iconographic searches.
