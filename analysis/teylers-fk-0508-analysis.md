## Object Description

The **Van Marum Large Electrostatic Generator** — the **largest flat-plate frictional electrostatic machine ever built**, and one of the most important surviving scientific instruments of the 18th century. It remains the centerpiece of the Teylers Museum instrument room. Two coaxial glass discs (1.65 m diameter each) rotate against friction cushions, generating static charge collected by brass combs and stored in a battery of approximately 100 Leyden jars across four complete sets — the largest Leyden jar battery ever assembled. Capable of generating approximately 330,000 V (sparks to 60 cm); combined with the full battery, voltages approaching 720 kV. **Van Marum first observed the smell of ozone using this machine in 1785**, though he did not identify the gas as such.

Commissioned by **Martinus van Marum** (1750–1837), first director of Teylers Museum, from instrument maker **John Cuthbertson** in May 1783. Cuthbertson was paid 3,250 guilders. The wooden support and cabinetwork were designed by architect **Leendert Viervant the Younger** (1752–**1801**). The machine was placed in the newly opened Oval Room on **Christmas Day (eerste kerstdag), 25 December 1784**. The inscription on the machine reads: *J. CUTHBERTSON Fecit Amsterdam MDCCLXXXIV*.

## Key Findings

### 1. LEENDERT VIERVANT DEATH YEAR WRONG: 1802 LISTED, CORRECT IS 1801

The catalog lists Viervant as "(1752-**1802**)." All authoritative sources — Wikipedia, Wikidata (Q474308), and genealogical records — consistently give his death date as **4 July 1801** in Amsterdam. The catalog is off by one year. This is a factual error in a creator's biographical dates.

### 2. VIERVANT'S ROLE "DESIGNER" IS MISLEADING

The catalog lists Viervant with the generic role "designer" alongside Van Marum's "designer/inventor," implying co-equal design responsibility for the instrument. In fact, Viervant designed only the **wooden support structure and cabinetwork** — he was the cabinetmaker/architect responsible for the mahogany table and supports, not the scientific instrument itself. This distinction is nowhere explained. Viervant was also the architect of the Teylers Oval Room (completed 1784) in which the machine was installed — a significant contextual connection also absent from the record.

### 3. PRODUCTION DATE "1783-1791" IS UNEXPLAINED AND MISLEADING

The catalog gives "1783-1791" without clarification. 1783 is when Cuthbertson was commissioned (May). The machine was complete and in service on **Christmas Day 1784** — which the date free-text field itself confirms. The machine's own inscription reads **MDCCLXXXIV (1784)**. "1791" refers to the date of the last known modification — not the original production. Without explanation, the range implies an 8-year construction that is simply not the case. The catalog's date field contradicts its own date free-text, which correctly states "eerste kerstdag 1784."

### 4. LEYDEN JAR BATTERY NOT DESCRIBED OR DIMENSIONED

The title refers to "Leidse flessen" (Leyden jars) but the catalog contains no quantification or description of the battery: approximately **100 jars across 4 complete sets**, each jar 13 inches in diameter and 2 feet tall, combined capacitor surface area of ~550 square feet — the largest Leyden jar battery ever assembled. The materials list gives "brass, glass, mahogany" without distinguishing machine from jars. No jar dimensions are recorded. The image shows only one set displayed.

### 5. NO DESCRIPTION OF FUNCTION OR SCIENTIFIC SIGNIFICANCE

No free-text description explaining what the machine does, what scientific principle it demonstrates, what Van Marum used it for, or why it is historically significant. For the world's largest surviving flat-plate electrostatic generator — directly connected to early high-voltage electricity research and the first observation of ozone (1785) — the absence of any description is a serious omission.

### 6. Inscription date (1784) not reconciled with date range (1783–1791)

The inscription "MDCCLXXXIV = 1784" on the machine itself is not cross-referenced to the date field "1783-1791" — the apparent contradiction is unresolved.

### 7. No primary bibliography

The foundational primary source — Van Marum's own two-volume *Beschryving eener ongemeen groote electrizeer-machine* (1785–1787) — is absent from any bibliography field. The modern multi-volume *Martinus van Marum: Life and Work* series is also unmentioned.

### 8. No provenance / acquisition cost

No acquisition date, method, or cost recorded. The machine was commissioned in 1783 and Cuthbertson was paid **3,250 guilders** — a documented historical fact absent from the record.

### 9. "Christmas Eve" vs "Christmas Day" discrepancy unresolved

The catalog correctly uses "eerste kerstdag" (Christmas Day = 25 December). Many English-language secondary sources say "Christmas Eve." The catalog does not cite Van Marum's 1785 publication as the primary source for this date, leaving the discrepancy unresolved in the literature.
