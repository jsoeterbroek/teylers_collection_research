## Object Description

The **second edition** of *A Journal of a Voyage to the South Seas, in His Majesty's Ship, the Endeavour* (London: Charles Dilly and James Phillips, 1784), by Sydney Parkinson (c.1745–1771). Parkinson served as botanical and natural history draughtsman to Sir Joseph Banks on Cook's first Pacific voyage (1768–1771). He died at sea on 26 January 1771 after contracting dysentery at Batavia. The first edition (1773) was edited controversially by his brother Stanfield Parkinson. This 1784 second edition was edited by **John Coakley Lettsom**, contains an extensive appendix (pp. [213]–353) with abridgements of Cook's second and third voyages, and includes 27 hand-coloured copper-engraved plates plus a portrait frontispiece and maps. The book is historically significant as one of the few unofficial accounts of Cook's first voyage, and the 1773 first edition contains the **first printed naming of the kangaroo** in any published book.

## Key Findings

### 1. WRONG PLATE COUNT: 29 IS INCORRECT — SHOULD BE 27

The catalog states the book "features 29 colored and engraved views." Multiple authoritative bibliographic sources (Hill 1309, Mitchell Library Cook 714, Sabin 58788; confirmed by SPL Rare Books, Donald Heald Rare Books, Forum Auctions) consistently describe **27 engraved plates** plus a separately counted portrait frontispiece and maps. The catalog appears to have conflated the portrait and/or maps with the plates to arrive at an inflated total of 29. This is a factual error in the physical description.

### 2. "WALLER" DOES NOT EXIST — SHOULD BE "WALLIS"

The catalog note names "Commanders Waller, Carteret, Cook, Clerke" among the voyage commanders in the appendix. **"Waller" is wrong.** The correct name is **Captain Samuel Wallis** (1728–1795), who commanded HMS Dolphin on its 1766–1768 circumnavigation and was the first European to reach Tahiti. Wallis is universally cited in all bibliographic descriptions of this appendix. "Waller" is a transcription error that misidentifies a major figure in the history of Pacific exploration.

### 3. TITLE IS TRUNCATED — FULL TITLE OMITS ESSENTIAL INFORMATION

The catalog records only *"A journal of a voyage to the South seas."* The full title is: *A Journal of a Voyage to the South Seas, in His Majesty's Ship the Endeavour, faithfully transcribed from the papers of the late Sydney Parkinson, draughtsman to Sir Joseph Banks Bart in his Expedition with Dr Solander round the World, to which is added Remarks on the Preface, by the late John Fothergill and appendix containing an Account of the Voyages of Commodore Byron, Captain Wallis, Captain Carteret, Monsieur Bougainville, Captain Cook and Captain Clerke.* The truncation omits the ship name (Endeavour), Parkinson's role as draughtsman, and the appendix description — all of which are essential for distinguishing this edition from the 1773 first edition.

### 4. EDITION NOT RECORDED

The catalog does not state that this is the **second edition** (the first appeared in 1773). The two editions differ substantially: the 1784 edition was edited by John Coakley Lettsom, includes additional maps showing all three Cook voyages, and contains the appendix (pp. [213]–353) on Cook's second and third voyages. Omitting the edition statement makes it impossible to distinguish this copy from the first edition without physically consulting the book.

### 5. EDITOR JOHN COAKLEY LETTSOM NOT MENTIONED

The 1784 second edition was prepared and edited by **John Coakley Lettsom** (1744–1815), a Quaker physician and botanist who was a close associate of Fothergill. Lettsom organised the supplementary appendix material and wrote additional editorial matter. The catalog mentions only Fothergill's foreword but omits Lettsom's editorial role entirely — a significant omission for the second edition specifically.

### 6. Banks and Solander listed as "contributors" — misleading

Neither Banks nor Solander contributed text to this book. Banks actively attempted to suppress the original 1773 publication. Solander died the year before the second edition appeared (1782). They are associated figures from the voyage documented by Parkinson, not contributors in the bibliographic sense.

### 7. Physical description incomplete

Pagination is deferred to annotation (never properly entered). The full pagination is: xxiii, [1], 212, [2], xxiii, 22, 353 pp. plus errata leaf. Physical dimensions are approximately 356 × 286 mm (quarto).

### 8. Landmark historical significance absent

No note records that the 1773 first edition (of which this is the second edition) contains the **first printed identification of the kangaroo by name** in any published book (p. 149). This is documented by the State Library of New South Wales, Donald Heald Rare Books, and multiple bibliographic sources.
