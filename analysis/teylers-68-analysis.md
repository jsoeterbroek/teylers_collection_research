## Object Description

This is the first critical edition of Ammonius's *De adfinium vocabulorum Differentia* (On the Differences of Synonymous Expressions), an ancient Greek grammatical treatise distinguishing near-synonyms. Edited by the young Lodewijk Caspar Valckenaer (1715–1785), who was only 24 at the time, and published by Jean Luzac in Leiden in 1739. The edition includes four previously unpublished ancient grammatical texts and three books of Valckenaer's own scholarly commentary. It was a landmark publication that launched Valckenaer's career as one of the greatest Dutch classical philologists.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. AUTHOR IDENTITY DISPUTED

The catalog attributes the work to "Ammonius" without qualification. Modern scholarship has established that:
- The **original author** was likely **Herennius Philo of Byblus** (c. AD 42–c. 130)
- The current text is a **revision by a later Byzantine editor** whose name may have been Ammonius
- He is NOT the same as Ammonius Hermiae (book 63 in this library) or any other well-known Ammonius
- The attribution question is itself a scholarly issue that the catalog should note

### 2. VALCKENAER'S SIGNIFICANCE NOT MENTIONED

**Lodewijk Caspar Valckenaer** (1715–1785) was one of the greatest Dutch classical philologists:
- Born in Leeuwarden, pupil of **Tiberius Hemsterhuis** at Franeker and Leiden
- This 1739 edition was his **first major publication**, completed at age 24
- Its success led directly to his appointment as **professor of Greek at Franeker** in 1741
- In 1765, succeeded Hemsterhuis in the **chair of Greek at Leiden**
- Later published major editions of Euripides and Theocritus

The catalog lists him only as "Valckenaer, L.C." without any context.

### 3. "OPUSCULA NONDUM EDITA" — FIRST EDITIONS OF FOUR TEXTS

The catalog does not explain the significance of the "opuscula nondum edita" (previously unpublished small works). Valckenaer published **four ancient texts for the first time**, from manuscripts in the Leiden University Library:
1. **Eranius Philo** — *De differentia significationis*
2. **Lesbonax** — *De figuris grammaticis*
3. **Anonymous texts** — *De soloecismo & barbarismo*
4. **Lexicon de spiritibus dictionum** — from works of Tryphon, Choeroboscus, and Theodoritus

This makes the volume effectively an **editio princeps** for multiple texts.

### 4. PUBLICATION PLACE: LEIDEN, NOT NIJMEGEN

"Lugduni Batavorum" is **Leiden** — the Latin name of Leiden University is *Academia Lugduno-Batava*. Any interpretation as Nijmegen is incorrect.

### 5. LUZAC PUBLISHER: SIGNIFICANT HUGUENOT DYNASTY

Jean Luzac senior (1702–1783) was part of a prominent **Huguenot publishing family** in Leiden, closely tied to the university. The family also published the internationally influential *Gazette de Leyde*. This publisher context is absent.

### 6. DIGITIZED COPIES EXIST ELSEWHERE

Despite the Teylers copy not being digitized, a copy is freely available on the **Internet Archive**. WorldCat lists it under OCLC 13607989 with holdings in multiple research libraries. A revised edition was published in 1822, demonstrating the work's lasting scholarly value.
