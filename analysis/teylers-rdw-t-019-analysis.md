## Object Description

A collaborative New Year's greeting collage on paper (330 x 412 mm) made in late **1970** by **Niki de Saint-Phalle** (Neuilly-sur-Seine, 1930 – La Jolla, 2002) and **Jean Tinguely** (Fribourg, 1925 – Bern, 1991), sent for New Year **1971** to **Edy de Wilde** (then director of the Stedelijk Museum Amsterdam) and his partner **Nono Reinhold**. Executed in collage, felt-tip marker, ballpoint pen, and adhesive tape. The inscription thanks de Wilde for dinner and invites the recipients to visit the couple at **Soisy-sur-Ecole** — their studio/residence 44 km southeast of Paris, also the site of their ongoing work on the monumental sculpture **Le Cyclop** (begun 1969). Donated to Teylers in 1998 via the **Stichting Reinhold De Wilde**. Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. RECIPIENT'S NAME MISSPELLED (MAJOR)

The catalog inscription field refers to **"Eddy de Wilde"**. The director's name was **Edy de Wilde** (Eduard Leon Louis de Wilde, 1919–2005) — he consistently used the spelling "Edy," not "Eddy." This error appears in the transcription and/or interpretation of the inscription and is a factual mistake in a field recording the name of a significant historical figure.

### 2. CREATORS LISTED ONLY AS "TEKENAAR" — MISREPRESENTS THE MEDIUM (MINOR)

The catalog lists both artists with the role **"tekenaar"** (draughtsman/drawer). However, collage is the primary technique of this work — cut-and-pasted paper elements, stickers, and adhesive tape — with felt-tip marker and ballpoint pen as secondary elements. Classifying both artists as "drawers" misrepresents their practice and may misdirect researchers looking for Saint-Phalle's collage works. A more accurate role would include **"collagist"** or **"mixed media."**

### 3. DATE AMBIGUITY NOT CLARIFIED (MINOR)

The record notes the work was produced in 1970 and dated 1971. Without explanation, this reads as an inconsistency. It should be explicitly noted that this is a **New Year's greeting card** made in December 1970 for the New Year of 1971 — the date "1971" is the year of greeting, not of production.

### 4. NO BIOGRAPHICAL CONTEXT FOR EITHER CREATOR (MAJOR)

The catalog provides only birth/death years. Key biographical context missing:

- **Niki de Saint-Phalle:** Born 29 October 1930 in Neuilly-sur-Seine; French-American artist; associated with **Nouveau Réalisme** (joined 1961 as its only female member); known for *Tirs* performances and **Nana** sculpture series; died 21 May 2002 in La Jolla, California.
- **Jean Tinguely:** Born 22 May 1925 in Fribourg; Swiss kinetic sculptor; co-founder of **Nouveau Réalisme** (original signatory 27 October 1960); known for metamechanical kinetic sculptures; died 30 August 1991 in Bern.
- Both artists married on **13 July 1971 in Soisy** — one year after this work was made. At time of creation they were an unmarried couple.

### 5. NO CONTEXT FOR THE RECIPIENT OR THE DONATING FOUNDATION (MAJOR)

The credit line names "Stichting Reinhold De Wilde" without explanation. Essential context:

- **Edy de Wilde** (1919–2005) was director of the **Stedelijk Museum Amsterdam from 1963 to 1985** — one of the most influential museum directors in 20th-century Dutch art history.
- **Nono Reinhold** (born 1929) was a graphic artist and de Wilde's partner. Together they built a collection of works on paper received as personal gifts from artists they knew internationally.
- The collection was donated to Teylers in **1998** and includes works by Roy Lichtenstein, Sol LeWitt, Armando, and others.
- The inscription thanking "de Wilde" for dinner documents a personal friendship that explains *why* the work exists — this provenance context is entirely absent.

### 6. NOUVEAU RÉALISME MOVEMENT NOT MENTIONED (MAJOR)

Both artists were central figures of **Nouveau Réalisme**, the French avant-garde movement founded in Paris in 1960. Tinguely was one of the nine original signatories of Pierre Restany's manifesto (27 October 1960); Saint-Phalle joined in 1961 as the only female member. This is the primary art-historical movement contextualizing their work and is entirely absent from the record.

### 7. "SOISY" REFERENCE NOT EXPLAINED — LE CYCLOP CONTEXT MISSING (MAJOR)

The inscription inviting de Wilde to visit **Soisy-sur-Ecole** is directly connected to the couple's active work on **Le Cyclop** (begun 1969) — a monumental 22.5-meter-high walk-in sculpture hidden in the forest near Milly-la-Forêt, adjacent to Soisy-sur-Ecole. In 1970–1971, when this work was made, Le Cyclop was under active construction. The catalog provides no context for this reference, leaving a key element of the inscription unexplained.

### 8. STEDELIJK MUSEUM CONNECTION TO BOTH ARTISTS NOT STATED (MAJOR)

The greeting was sent to the director of the Stedelijk Museum, with whom both artists had a significant institutional history:
- Niki de Saint-Phalle had her **first major museum exhibition** at the Stedelijk in **1967** ("Les Nanas au pouvoir")
- Jean Tinguely had major Stedelijk exhibitions: **1961** ("Bewogen Beweging") and **1962** ("Dylaby")

The work documents a specific friendship within the international art world — a dimension entirely absent from the catalog.
