## Object Description

A large-format finished drawing on paper (251 x 410 mm) by **Adriaen van Ostade** (Haarlem, 1610 – Haarlem, 1685), dated **1673**, depicting a tavern interior with peasants drinking and dancing: drinkers at a table (left), a dancing couple (center), furniture (right), and musicians near a fireplace (background). Executed in pen in brown, watercolor wash, opaque paint, and pen in gold — the gold framing lines being a distinctive feature of Van Ostade's autonomous presentation drawings. Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. ARTIST DEATH YEAR WRONG (MAJOR)

The catalog states **Adriaen van Ostade (1610–1684)**. The correct death year is **1685**. Van Ostade died on **27 April 1685** and was buried on **2 May 1685** at St. Bavo's Church in Haarlem. This is confirmed by Britannica, the National Gallery London, the J. Paul Getty Museum, Art UK, the NGA Washington, and archival burial records. The error "1684" appears to derive from a Dutch library authority file and has propagated into the Teylers catalog.

### 2. IRRELEVANT BIBLIOGRAPHY ENTRY ATTACHED TO THIS RECORD (MAJOR)

The first bibliography entry, **"aquarel: Nederlandse meesters van de negentiende eeuw"** (Van Druten, Van Dijk, Sillevis; Uitgeverij THOTH / Teylers Museum, 2015), is a catalog for a **2015 Teylers exhibition of 19th-century Dutch watercolor artists** — Mesdag, Mauve, Israëls, Breitner, Mondriaan, and others. It has zero scholarly relevance to a 1673 drawing by a Golden Age master. This is almost certainly a **database linking error** in which a bibliographic record was accidentally attached to the wrong object. A bibliography entry that actively contradicts the object's period (17th century ≠ 19th century) is a serious cataloging failure that will mislead researchers.

### 3. SCHNACKENBURG CATALOGUE RAISONNÉ ABSENT (MAJOR)

The essential scholarly reference for all Van Ostade drawings — **Bernhard Schnackenburg, *Adriaen van Ostade / Isack van Ostade: Zeichnungen und Aquarelle*, 2 vols. (Hamburg, 1981)** — is entirely absent from the bibliography. This is the catalogue raisonné for Van Ostade's drawings and watercolors, and every serious museum record for a Van Ostade drawing cites it. Its absence makes scholarly cross-referencing impossible from the online record.

### 4. NO PROVENANCE (MAJOR)

The catalog records no provenance whatsoever for this flagship Topstuk. No acquisition date, no source, no prior collection history. For a large-format, signed-and-dated, autonomous presentation drawing by one of the most collected Dutch Golden Age masters, this is a serious omission by any standard.

### 5. BIBLIOGRAPHY ENTRY HAS WRONG CAPITALIZATION / FORMATTING (MINOR)

The third bibliography entry reads **"dutch Drawings in the Teyler Museum"** — with "dutch" in lower case. The correct title is **"Dutch Drawings in the Teyler Museum, II: Artists Born Between 1575 and 1630"** (M.C. Plomp, Haarlem/Ghent, 1997). The error is typographic but signals insufficient quality control in bibliographic data entry.

### 6. CREATOR ROLE "TEKENAAR" MISREPRESENTS THE ARTIST (MINOR)

Van Ostade is classified only as **"tekenaar"** (draughtsman). He was primarily a **painter** and highly prolific **printmaker** (etcher), with drawings and watercolors representing only one strand of his output. This classification is particularly misleading for a watercolor/pen work that, despite being on paper, was made as an autonomous presentation piece — not a study or preparatory drawing.

### 7. "PEN IN GOUD" NOT EXPLAINED (MINOR)

The gold pen lines (framing lines in gold ink around the composition) are noted in the technique field but not explained in the description. This is a distinctive feature of Van Ostade's finished presentation drawings — he used gold framing lines to signal that the work was a complete autonomous piece intended for a collector album, not a studio study. This context is absent.

### 8. NO ART-HISTORICAL CONTEXT (MINOR)

No explanation of the work's significance:
- This is a **large-format late-period work** from 1673, when Van Ostade was 62 — an age at which he was producing highly refined autonomous drawings for the most discerning collectors
- The subject matter — peasant interior scenes — is the genre for which Van Ostade is most celebrated, representing the culmination of a tradition begun by Adriaen Brouwer
- No mention of comparable works in other collections (Rijksmuseum, British Museum, Hamburg) or of Van Ostade's place in the history of Dutch genre drawing
