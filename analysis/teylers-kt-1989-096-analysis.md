## Object Description

A small portrait drawing on white paper (175 × 126 mm), in black medium, depicting **August Friedrich Schmidt** (11 February 1795, Anklam, Pomerania – 12 September 1899, Wolgast, Pomerania) — celebrated at his death as the **last surviving German veteran of the Napoleonic Wars of Liberation (1813–1815)** and, by many accounts, **the last surviving combatant of the Battle of Waterloo** of any nationality. Schmidt enlisted as a volunteer Jäger in the 1st Pomeranian Infantry Regiment on 17 March 1813 and fought at Bautzen, Großbeeren, Dennewitz, Leipzig, Laon, Ligny, and Waterloo. He worked as a goldsmith in Wolgast and died seven months after his 104th birthday.

The drawing shows Schmidt in bust format, wearing three medals and a soft house cap, with the inscription *"August Schmidt / der letzte Freiheitskämpfer von 1813–15"* below. Accompanying the drawing are two sheets of handwritten German text in Kurrent script sourced from **Über Land und Meer** (Stuttgart illustrated weekly), No. 52, 1898–1899, with the portrait published with permission of the Deutschen Kriegerbund-Buchhandlung, Tempelhof-Berlin. The object was acquired in 1989 from the collection formerly belonging to **Adriaan van der Willigen Pz.** (1810–1876), Haarlem physician, art historian, and member of Teylers Tweede Genootschap.

## Key Findings

### 1. ONLINE IMAGES 01 AND 02 ARE THE WRONG OBJECTS

The EXIF metadata embedded in the image files served as KT 1989 096-01.jpg and KT 1989 096-02.jpg identifies them as **KT 1989 084** (portrait, recto) and **KT 1989 083 verso** — entirely different catalog objects. Only images 03 and 04 are correctly identified as KT 1989 096. This is a digital asset management error that causes the online record to display photographs of the wrong objects and misrepresents what this drawing looks like to any online researcher.

### 2. "FREIHEITSKMÄMPFER" IS A NONSENSE WORD — TRANSCRIPTION ERROR

The inscription field records *"der letzte Freiheitskmämpfer von 1813–15"* — an impossible German compound with an inserted 'k'. The inscription on the drawing itself reads *"Freiheitskämpfer"* (freedom fighter). This is a transcription error introduced by catalog staff.

### 3. SUBJECT NAME MISSPELLED AS "SMIDT"

The subject tag reads *"portret (Smidt, August (104 jr.))"* — spelling "Schmidt" as "Smidt." All historical sources, the inscription on the drawing, and Wikipedia/Wikidata confirm the surname is **Schmidt**. This misspelling in the index tag impairs discoverability in the catalog database.

### 4. "102" IN INSCRIPTION FIELD CONTRADICTS ALL KNOWN FACTS

The catalog inscription field records a notation "102." Schmidt died at 104 (born 11 February 1795, died 12 September 1899). The catalog's own title field and subject tag both correctly state "104-jarige." The "102" is either a misread or phantom notation that contradicts verified historical data.

### 5. TECHNIQUE MISIDENTIFIED AS "ZWART KRIJT" (BLACK CHALK)

The catalog records "zwart krijt" (black chalk). Visual analysis of the drawing shows characteristics consistent with **graphite pencil** — fine detail lines, soft tonal gradations, a slightly waxy quality. Black chalk produces more granular, friable, matte marks. Misidentification of medium affects conservation handling and scholarly reference.

### 6. "ANONIEM NL" (ANONYMOUS DUTCH) LIKELY INCORRECT

The source is documented in the handwritten notes as the German illustrated magazine *Über Land und Meer* (Stuttgart), published with permission of the Deutschen Kriegerbund-Buchhandlung, Tempelhof-Berlin. The artist is more likely **German**, not Dutch. The attribution "Anoniem NL" has no documented basis.

### 7. Subject tag "100-jarige" is imprecise

One subject tag reads "portret (100-jarige)" — centenarian. The title itself specifies he was 104. "100-jarige" is technically not wrong but inconsistent with the title field and the second subject tag "104 jr."

### 8. Published source not recorded in bibliography

The drawing's origin is directly traceable to *Über Land und Meer*, No. 52, 1898–1899, as stated in the handwritten text in the object itself. The bibliography field is blank.

### 9. Van der Willigen provenance not explained

"Collectie A. van der Willigen" is noted without identifying **Adriaan van der Willigen Pz.** (1810–1876), physician, art historian, and Teylers Tweede Genootschap member. His collection was sold at auction in The Hague (A.G. de Visser) on 12 August 1874. How the drawing reached Teylers in 1989 — 115 years after that auction — is undocumented.

### 10. Historical significance of subject entirely absent

No note explains who August Schmidt was: the last surviving combatant of the Battle of Waterloo. His death in September 1899 generated widespread international press coverage. This context is essential for understanding why the drawing was made, collected, and preserved.
