## Object Description

A drawing on paper (304 x 353 mm) by **Andy Warhol** (Pittsburgh, 1928 – New York, 1987), dated c. 1953, depicting two hands from different people bound together with handcuffs. Executed in pencil, pen in Indian ink, and white opaque paint. The work belongs to a group of approximately 300 drawings discovered in **2011** in Warhol's estate flat files by Munich dealer **Daniel Blau** — all from Warhol's 1950s commercial illustration years, many drawn after source photographs in *LIFE* magazine. Acquired by Teylers in 2016 with support from the VriendenLoterij. Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. COPYRIGHT CREDITS WRONG ORGANIZATION (MAJOR)

The catalog states **"© Pictoright Amsterdam"** as the copyright holder. This is factually incorrect. The copyright to all Warhol works is held by **The Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts, Inc.**, with **Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York** as its exclusive licensing representative worldwide. Pictoright is a Dutch collective rights management intermediary — not the copyright owner. The correct credit line is:

> *© [year] The Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts, Inc. / Licensed by Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York*

Naming Pictoright as the copyright holder — rather than as a Dutch representative — is a significant institutional error that misidentifies who owns the rights.

### 2. ESTATE DISCOVERY CONTEXT ENTIRELY ABSENT (MAJOR)

The catalog acknowledges that the date comes from "documentatie Daniel Blau" but provides no explanation of what this means. The full context:

- In **2011**, Munich gallery owner **Daniel Blau** discovered approximately **300 previously unknown drawings** in Warhol's estate flat files — all from his 1950s commercial illustration phase in New York
- The group was published in *From Silverpoint to Silver Screen: Andy Warhol 1950s Drawings* (Hirmer, 2012/2013) and toured as an exhibition
- **Teylers Museum itself hosted this exhibition** (2 June – 1 September 2013)
- KT 2016 092 was acquired in 2016, from the same corpus

Without this context, a researcher encountering the catalog entry cannot understand the work's provenance or dating methodology.

### 3. NO PROVENANCE CHAIN (MAJOR)

The catalog records no provenance. The chain should be:
1. Warhol's studio, New York (c. 1953)
2. Andy Warhol estate / Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts, New York (1987–2011, in flat files)
3. Discovered by Daniel Blau, Munich, 2011
4. Daniel Blau gallery, Munich (sold to Teylers Museum, 2016)

This provenance chain is not only contextually important — for works from unrecognized estate holdings, it is essential for authentication and due diligence.

### 4. NO EXHIBITION HISTORY (MAJOR)

No exhibitions are recorded. Teylers Museum hosted the *From Silverpoint to Silver Screen* exhibition (June–September 2013) presenting this corpus. Whether this specific drawing was shown then, the record is silent.

### 5. KEY SCHOLARLY PUBLICATIONS NOT CITED (MAJOR)

No bibliography is given. Essential references:
- *From Silverpoint to Silver Screen: Andy Warhol 1950s Drawings* (Hirmer, 2012/2013, ed. James Hofmaier and Sydney Picasso) — the exhibition catalog for the corpus discovery
- *Andy Warhol: The LIFE Years 1949–1959* (Hirmer, 2016, ed. Paul Tanner / ETH Zürich) — the follow-up scholarly publication, published in the same year as Teylers' acquisition
- Andy Warhol Catalogue Raisonné (Phaidon, ongoing) — should be noted as **not covering** 1950s drawings (volumes cover only 1961 onward)

### 6. OBJECT NAME FIELD MISUSED (MAJOR)

The "Object Name" field reads "pen in inkt" — a technique description, not an object type. The field should read "tekening" (drawing), which is already correctly listed in the separate "Object Category" field. This creates redundancy and misuses the field.

### 7. DATING METHODOLOGY NOT EXPLAINED (MINOR)

The date "ca. 1953" is acknowledged as deriving from Daniel Blau's documentation. The methodology — stylistic comparison and source photograph identification from *LIFE* magazine — is not described. The catalog should note that no formal catalogue raisonné covers Warhol's 1950s drawings and that the Blau documentation represents the only published scholarly dating basis.

### 8. "ESTATE OF ANDY WARHOL" INSCRIPTION NOT DESCRIBED (MINOR)

The inscription field lists "The estate of Andy Warhol" without specifying whether this is a rubber stamp, ink label, handwritten note, or printed sticker; its exact text; or its location (verso). Works from this discovered group bear estate authentication stamps on the verso — the catalog should specify this.

### 9. BLOTTED LINE TECHNIQUE NOT ADDRESSED (MINOR)

Warhol's characteristic 1950s working method — the "blotted line" transfer technique (inking a pencil drawing on tracing paper and blotting it onto a second sheet) — is not mentioned. Whether this specific work uses that method or is a direct drawing should be noted in the technical description.

### 10. SOURCE PHOTOGRAPH NOT IDENTIFIED (MINOR)

For the corpus of estate drawings, Daniel Blau's team identified source photographs from *LIFE* and other magazines for many works. Whether a source photograph has been identified for the handcuffs image is not stated.
