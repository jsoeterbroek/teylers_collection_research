## Object Description

A small **silverpoint** (*metaalstift*) portrait drawing on prepared parchment (vellum), 45 × 33 mm, by **Hendrick Goltzius** (1558–1617). It depicts **Jacques de la Faille** (c.1548–1615), a wealthy Antwerp merchant and Colonel of the Antwerp Civic Guard, and was made as a **preparatory study** (*tafelette*) for the engraved portrait New Hollstein 216, published in Haarlem by Harmen Adolfsz. in 1580, on the occasion of de la Faille's marriage to Josina Hamels. The inscription on the drawing itself reads *"voor de prent"* (for the print), explicitly confirming its preparatory function. De la Faille later fled Spanish-controlled Antwerp for Haarlem in 1584. The finished engraving is held in the Rijksmuseum, Philadelphia, Princeton, the Art Institute of Chicago, and Musea Brugge. The Teylers drawing belongs to a group of nearly one hundred surviving Goltzius *silverpoint tafeletten* — the largest such surviving group anywhere in the world.

## Key Findings

### 1. "GRAVURE GEDATEERD 1580" IN A DRAWING RECORD — INTERNALLY INCOHERENT

The date free-text field reads **"gravure gedateerd 1580"** ("engraving dated 1580"). This is a date field for a **drawing**, not an engraving. The catalog sourced its date from the finished print and preserved the "gravure" reference verbatim in the drawing record. The object is classified as "tekening" (drawing) using "metaalstift" (metalpoint) technique — yet the date field references an engraving. This is internally contradictory. The date of the drawing should read "c.1580 (preparatory to the engraving)."

### 2. SUBJECT BIRTH DATE MISSING — ONLY DEATH YEAR IN TITLE

The title records "Jacques de la Faille (gest. 1615)" — death year only, no birth date anywhere in the record. De la Faille was born **c.1548**; his full dates c.1548–1615 are given consistently by the Philadelphia Museum of Art, Princeton University Art Museum, Rijksmuseum, Musea Brugge, and the Art Institute of Chicago. The birth date is essential for understanding the subject's age (~32) at the time of the portrait and its marriage commemoration context.

### 3. ARTIST NATIONALITY NOT RECORDED

Goltzius's nationality is absent. Hendrick Goltzius was **German-born** (Mühlbracht/Bracht-am-Niederrhein, Duchy of Jülich, now North Rhine-Westphalia) and settled permanently in Haarlem from 1577. Authoritative sources (Metropolitan Museum, Wikipedia, WGA) consistently describe him as a Dutch graphic artist of German descent. This nuanced but important biographical datum is entirely absent.

### 4. Preparatory function not explained despite inscription confirming it

The inscription *"voor de prent"* (for the print) is recorded but the catalog contains no description field to explain what this means. The preparatory function, the finished engraving (New Hollstein 216), the publisher (Harmen Adolfsz.), and the companion portrait of Josina Hamels are all absent. A researcher consulting this record cannot understand the drawing's relationship to the print.

### 5. Subject's occupation and context entirely absent

Jacques de la Faille was **Colonel of the Antwerp Civic Guard** and a prominent merchant. The portrait was made for his **marriage to Josina Hamels in 1580**. He **fled Antwerp for Haarlem in 1584** after the Spanish reconquest. None of this context appears in the catalog. The subject field lists only generic terms.

### 6. No prose description

No descriptive text anywhere in the record — no description of the sitter's pose, dress, or the physical character of the drawing.

### 7. Silverpoint not specified — "metaalstift" too generic

Goltzius used **silverpoint** (*zilverstift*) for virtually all his surviving *tafeletten*, confirmed by NGA's "Drawing in Silver and Gold" exhibition (2015) and the CODART metalpoint survey. The catalog uses the broader "metaalstift," which is not wrong but less precise than scholarship supports.

### 8. No provenance information

No acquisition history recorded. The provenance of Teylers' Goltzius holdings is historically documented and significant.
