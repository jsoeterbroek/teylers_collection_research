## Object Description

A **heliostat** (*zonnevolger*) built on the design of German astronomer **Wilhelm Klinkerfues** (1827–1884) of the Göttingen Observatory, manufactured by Göttingen precision instrument maker **Meyerstein**, dated 1865. A heliostat is an instrument equipped with a clock-driven mirror that continuously rotates to compensate for the Earth's rotation, reflecting a steady beam of sunlight in a fixed direction — used in spectroscopy, photometry, and optical experiments requiring a stable light source.

Klinkerfues, professor of astronomy and director of the Göttingen Observatory from 1868, designed a distinctive heliostat type and published the design in *Astronomische Nachrichten*. The designation "naar Klinkerfues" (after Klinkerfues) in the title confirms this instrument implements his published design. Meyerstein was the leading precision instrument workshop in Göttingen in the mid-19th century, supplying the university's physics and astronomy departments.
