## Object Description

FK 0219 is a **gasometer** (*gazometer*) — a gas-measuring and gas-delivery apparatus — designed by **Martinus van Marum** (1750–1837) and built by instrument maker **Frederik Willem Fries** at Teylers Museum, Haarlem, in late 1790 to early 1791. It was specifically designed to demonstrate the combustion of hydrogen in oxygen, producing water and thereby confirming Lavoisier's proof that water is a compound (H₂O), not an element.

The device is van Marum's deliberate simplification and improvement of Lavoisier's original gasometer design (published in *Traité élémentaire de chimie*, 1789), built by Paris maker Pierre Mégnié and notoriously expensive. Van Marum's version made the water-synthesis demonstration affordable and reproducible for scientific institutions worldwide. Upon its completion van Marum received replication requests from scientific academies across Europe. Van Marum published its description in *Verhandelingen uitgegeven door Teyler's Tweede Genootschap*, Volume 10 (1798) — a primary technical source entirely absent from the catalog.

Fries was a Swiss-born instrument maker recruited by van Marum from London in 1790. He was introduced to Teylers' Second Society on 19 November 1790 and had left Haarlem by April 1791 due to homesickness — the tight production date "1790–1791" is fully confirmed by primary records.

## Key Findings

### 1. LAVOISIER LISTED AS "INVENTOR" OF THIS SPECIFIC INSTRUMENT — MISLEADING

The catalog assigns Lavoisier the role of **inventor** of FK 0219. This is misleading. Lavoisier invented the *original* gasometer concept. FK 0219 is an explicitly redesigned, simplified, and improved version by van Marum and Fries. Weiss (2013, p. 111) confirms van Marum "improved on Lavoisier's gasometer with Fries." The instrument the catalog implies is a Lavoisier work is in fact a Dutch innovation. Lavoisier should be credited as "inventor of original concept," not inventor of this object.

### 2. OBJECT NAME "VERBRANDINGSTOESTEL" MISCHARACTERISES THE INSTRUMENT TYPE

The catalog object name is "verbrandingstoestel waterstof" (hydrogen combustion apparatus). The instrument is primarily a **gasometer** (*gazometer*) — a device that stores, measures, and controls gas flow. The 1952 *Chemisch Weekblad* article explicitly calls it "de **gazometer** voor de verbranding van waterstof," making clear the device type is gasometer and combustion is the *experiment performed with* it. By naming it "verbrandingstoestel," the catalog obscures the instrument type and breaks comparability with international museum databases (e.g., Smithsonian NMAH CH.330605: "Lavoisier Gazometer").

### 3. NO DIMENSIONS RECORDED

No height, width, depth, or weight. Dimensions are essential for object identification, comparison with related instruments (the Smithsonian replica is 230 × 71 × 112 cm), and exhibition/storage planning. Their absence is a major documentation gap for a standard mandatory field.

### 4. NO PROVENANCE OR ACQUISITION HISTORY

No acquisition information. From historical records, van Marum commissioned this instrument for Teylers Museum in 1790–1791, specifically recruiting Fries from London for the purpose. The complete commission history is documented in the Teylers archives and Weiss (2013) but is entirely absent from the catalog record.

### 5. DESCRIPTION IS ONE NON-INFORMATIVE SENTENCE

The entire description reads: *"Verbrandingstoestel voor waterstof van Martinus van Marum."* This merely restates the title plus the designer's name. It adds no functional explanation, no scientific significance, no historical context, and no reference to van Marum's 1798 publication. It is functionally useless for research.

### 6. NO BIBLIOGRAPHY — VAN MARUM'S 1798 PUBLICATION ABSENT

Van Marum published a detailed description of this instrument in *Verhandelingen van Teyler's Tweede Genootschap*, Vol. 10 (1798) — the primary published source by the designer himself. The standard scholarly reference, Turner & Levere, *Martinus van Marum: Life and Work*, Vol. IV (1967), catalogued all instruments in the collection. Neither source appears anywhere in the record.

### 7. MAKER'S NAME IN WRONG LANGUAGE FORM

The catalog uses **"Frédéric Guillaume Fries"** (French rendering). All scholarly sources use **"Frederik Willem Fries"** (Dutch). Since Fries was Swiss, the French form is not obviously more correct, and it is inconsistent with the primary literature. The catalog should standardise on the Dutch form or note both variants.

### 8. IMAGE METADATA CONTRADICTS CATALOG CATEGORY

The object image's XMP metadata (Microsoft Expression Media CatalogSets) classifies the object as "Fysisch" (Physics). The catalog records "chemische apparatuur" (Chemical apparatus). The FK prefix itself stands for *Fysisch Kabinet*. Three conflicting classifications exist in Teylers' own records for this one object.
