## Object Description

A large watercolor and gouache on parchment (575 x 430 mm) by **Pauline Rifer de Courcelles** (Paris, 1781 – 1851), also known professionally as **Madame Knip**, dated 1811, depicting a male **Red Bird-of-Paradise** (*Paradisaea rubra* Daudin, 1800) perched on a branch. Made at the peak of the artist's career — the same year as her celebrated publication *Les Pigeons* (with Coenraad Jacob Temminck). Purchased at **Christie's Paris, 22 November 2022, lot 1** (hammer price €80,000; total with buyer's premium €100,800), with support from a named foundation (see credit line discrepancy below). Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**.

**Family connection:** Pauline Rifer de Courcelles was the stepmother of **Henriëtte Ronner-Knip** (1821–1909), the cat painter whose work is also in the Teylers Topstukken collection (KS 135).

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. CREDIT LINE MAY NAME THE WRONG FOUNDATION (MAJOR)

The catalog credits the **Don Quichote Foundation** as the supporting funder. However, the contemporaneous acquisition report in **La Tribune de l'Art** (published at the time of the November 2022 Christie's sale) states the acquisition was made with support of the **Brook Foundation** ("grâce au soutien de la Fondation Brook"). These are entirely different organisations. One source is the museum's own catalog; the other is a contemporaneous press report. This discrepancy requires urgent verification against the museum's acquisition records. If the credit line is wrong, this is a **material factual error** in the catalog's most basic record of institutional acknowledgment.

### 2. "FEM. [?] KNIP" — QUESTION MARK IS UNWARRANTED (MINOR)

The catalog transcribes the inscription with a "[?]" after "fem." — implying uncertainty about this abbreviation. In fact, "fem." is a standard 19th-century French convention abbreviating "femme" (wife). After her marriage to Dutch painter **Josephus Augustus Knip** (1777–1847) in 1808, Pauline consistently signed her works "Pauline de Courcelles femme Knip" or "fem. Knip." The Christie's catalog for this very lot confirms the transcription without any uncertainty: *"Signé Pauline / De Courcelles / fem. Knip et distinctement daté de 1811."* The "[?]" is an error of cataloguing judgment.

### 3. PROFESSIONAL NAME "MADAME KNIP" / "PAULINE KNIP" NOT CROSS-REFERENCED (MINOR)

The catalog lists the creator only as "Pauline Rifer de Courcelles." Her standard library authority name (Library of Congress: "Knip, Pauline, 1781-1851") and the name under which she is commonly cited in ornithological and art-historical literature — **Madame Knip** or **Pauline Knip** — are absent. A researcher searching under "Knip" will not find this record.

### 4. ROLE "TEKENAAR" UNDERSTATES HER STATUS (MINOR)

She was appointed **"Premier Peintre d'Histoire Naturelle de S. M. l'Impératrice Reine Marie-Louise"** — First Painter of Natural History to Empress Marie-Louise of Austria. Describing her only as "tekenaar" (draughtsman) is significantly reductive. Contemporary documents and Salon submissions consistently describe her as a peintre (painter).

### 5. NO PROVENANCE (MAJOR)

The catalog contains no provenance. At minimum the auction record must be documented: **Christie's Paris, Exceptional Sale, 22 November 2022, lot 1** (estimate €40,000–60,000; hammer price €80,000; total with buyer's premium €100,800). Pre-auction provenance is not publicly available and should be noted as unknown.

### 6. NO BIOGRAPHICAL CONTEXT (MAJOR)

The catalog gives only a name and lifespan. Major omissions include:
- Training under **Jacques Barraband** (1768–1809), the foremost French natural history painter
- **Gold medal at the 1810 Salon**, with prize of lodgings at the Sorbonne
- Appointment as **"Premier Peintre d'Histoire Naturelle"** to Empress Marie-Louise
- Collaboration with Dutch naturalist **Coenraad Jacob Temminck** on *Les Pigeons* (1808–1811) — one of the most celebrated ornithological publications of the Napoleonic era
- Work for the **Manufacture Nationale de Sèvres** decorating porcelain with bird subjects
- Marriage to **Josephus Augustus Knip** (1808, divorced 1824)
- **Stepmother of Henriëtte Ronner-Knip** (1821–1909) — the cat painter also in the Teylers Topstukken collection (KS 135)

### 7. NO ART-HISTORICAL OR SCIENTIFIC SIGNIFICANCE STATED (MAJOR)

The work was made in 1811, the same year as the publication of *Les Pigeons* — at the absolute peak of the artist's career. The species *Paradisaea rubra* was described by Daudin only in 1800, making this an early scientific illustration of a recently named species. The drawing exemplifies the Napoleonic French natural history tradition of combining scientific accuracy with high pictorial ambition, drawing on specimens at the Muséum d'Histoire Naturelle in Paris.

### 8. NO BIBLIOGRAPHY CITED (MAJOR)

No scholarly references are given. Essential sources:
- C.J. Temminck and Pauline Knip, *Les Pigeons* (Paris, 1808–1811)
- A.G. Desmarest and Pauline de Courcelles, *Histoire Naturelle des Tangaras, des Manakins et des Todiers* (Paris, 1805–1807)
- RKD entry for the artist: <https://rkd.nl/en/explore/artists/18746>
- Smithsonian Libraries blog: "Publishing, Pretense, and Pigeons: The Case of Madame Knip" (2017)

### 9. SCIENTIFIC NAME OF SPECIES ABSENT (MINOR)

The subject is described only as "dier: paradijsvogel." The species depicted is the **Red Bird-of-Paradise**, *Paradisaea rubra* Daudin, 1800 (historical synonym: *Uranornis rubra*). No scientific name is given, which is a significant omission for a natural history subject.

### 10. SEX OF THE DEPICTED BIRD NOT NOTED (MINOR)

*Paradisaea rubra* is strongly sexually dimorphic: the spectacular crimson flank plumes are male-only. The depicted bird is almost certainly a **male** — natural history illustrations of this period invariably depicted adult males as the visually striking sex. This should be stated in the catalog.
