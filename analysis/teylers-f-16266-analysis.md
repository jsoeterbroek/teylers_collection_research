## Object Description

F 16266 is the skull of a woolly mammoth (*Mammuthus primigenius*), discovered in 1820 near Heukelum in the Dutch province of Gelderland, and today known as "De Mammoetschedel van Heukelum." It is the largest and most complete mammoth skull found in the Netherlands, and one of the most complete of its kind in Western Europe. The animal was a male estimated at approximately 43 years of age at the time of death, and lived approximately 31,000 years ago in the Late Pleistocene (Upper Palaeolithic).

The skull was discovered on 26 January 1820 when a dike breach near Heukelum, along the Nieuwe Zuider Lingedijk, carved a 20-metre-deep hole in the land of farmer Frans van Wilgen, exposing the specimen. Museum director Martinus van Marum visited in March 1820, but van Wilgen was absent. The skull was sold at auction in 1824, purchased by the Hollandsche Maatschappij der Wetenschappen for 2,755 guilders — a sum equivalent to the cost of three houses at the time. It remained in the Hollandsche Maatschappij's Naturaliënkabinet until that collection was dissolved in 1866, after which the skull was held in storage until transfer to Teylers Museum in **1885**.

The catalog image shows the skull mounted on a wall bracket in the museum, with the two massive curved tusks displayed hanging beneath it. The skull retains both orbital regions, the frontal and parietal bones, and shows the characteristic domed cranium of *Mammuthus primigenius*. It is displayed in the First Fossil Hall.

## Key Findings

### 1. SCIENTIFIC NAME AUTHORITY AND YEAR MISSING

The catalog gives the scientific name only as "Mammuthus primigenius" with no authority or year. The correct, complete scientific name is ***Mammuthus primigenius* (Blumenbach, 1799)**. Johann Friedrich Blumenbach originally described the woolly mammoth in 1799 as *Elephas primigenius*; when placed in the genus *Mammuthus* (Brookes, 1828), the original author and date are retained in parentheses. This is standard zoological nomenclature. The GBIF Backbone Taxonomy confirms: *Mammuthus primigenius* (Blumenbach, 1799).

### 2. COLLECTION PLACE CONTAINS AN ERROR

The catalog records the collection place as "Heukelom, Z v.Gennep/Maas : ZLi : NL." This contains two problems:
- The correct modern spelling of the town is **Heukelum** (not Heukelom). The municipality of Heukelum is in the province of **Gelderland** (now part of West Betuwe municipality), not in Limburg (ZLi). The locality code "ZLi" (Zuid-Limburg, South Limburg) is incorrect.
- The skull was found along the Linge river, not near the Maas/Gennep area. Heukelum is located in the Bommelerwaard region (Gelderland), approximately 20 km south of Gorinchem, far from Gennep or the Maas. The confusion "Z v.Gennep/Maas" is a significant geographic error — the Maas does not flow through that region; the specimen comes from the Linge river valley area.

### 3. DESCRIPTION ENTIRELY MISSING

The catalog provides no narrative description of this object. The following scientifically relevant information is entirely absent:
- The skull is the **largest and most complete woolly mammoth skull found in the Netherlands**
- The individual was a **43-year-old male**
- It dates to approximately **31,000 years BP** (Upper Palaeolithic, MIS 3)
- It is a **subfossil** rather than a mineralised fossil (the bone retains original organic material)
- The specimen played a significant role in 19th-century debates about extinction and catastrophism
- Mammoth skulls are exceptionally rare as complete specimens because the porous, pneumatised cranium fractures easily when buried

### 4. ACQUISITION HISTORY ENTIRELY ABSENT

The catalog provides no provenance or acquisition history. The documented history is:
- Discovered **26 January 1820** following a dike breach near Heukelum (Nieuwe Zuider Lingedijk)
- Found by farmer **Frans van Wilgen** on his land
- Visited by Teylers Museum director **Martinus van Marum** in March 1820 (but no purchase was made at that time)
- Sold at auction in **1824** in Gorinchem
- Purchased by the **Hollandsche Maatschappij der Wetenschappen** for **2,755 guilders**
- Held in the Hollandsche Maatschappij's Naturaliënkabinet, Haarlem
- Transferred to Teylers Museum in **1885** after the Naturaliënkabinet was dissolved (1866)

This complex chain of custody is entirely absent from the catalog.

### 5. OBJECT CATEGORY IS IMPRECISE

The catalog assigns the category "Subfossiel tot recent" (Subfossil to Recent). While "subfossil" is technically defensible (Pleistocene bone that has not been fully permineralised), the category "tot recent" (to Recent) is misleading — the specimen is approximately 31,000 years old and cannot reasonably be described as Recent. The category conflates a Late Pleistocene mammoth skull with Holocene and modern biological material.

### 6. NO DIMENSIONS RECORDED

No dimensions are given. The skull is described in sources as the **largest mammoth skull in the Netherlands**, which makes dimensions a critical piece of information. Published literature on Dutch Pleistocene mammals (including the docplayer.nl article on the skull's history and the Mijn Gelderland description) provides measurements.

### 7. CANONICAL STATUS NOT NOTED

Like the Mosasaurus (F 07424), the Heukelum mammoth skull is one of four Teylers specimens designated as part of the **Canon van Nederland**. This is not noted in the catalog record.

### 8. NO BIBLIOGRAPHY

No literature references are provided. The specimen has its own dedicated historical literature, including a documented publication history in the *Archives du Musée Teyler* and later analyses of Dutch Pleistocene mammoth finds.
