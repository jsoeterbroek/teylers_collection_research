## Object Description

This is a cast silver medal depicting Jan Hus (c. 1369/1370–1415), the Czech religious reformer who was burned at the stake as a heretic at the Council of Constance on 6 July 1415. The obverse shows Hus's portrait bust; the reverse depicts his execution with Latin inscriptions including his famous prophecy. The medal belongs to the well-documented "Hustaler" tradition of Reformation commemorative medals. Despite the "1415" date inscribed on the medal, it was **not manufactured in 1415** — it is a later commemorative piece, most likely from the early 18th century (c. 1700–1740). It was part of the personal collection of Pieter Teyler van der Hulst, the museum's founder.

## Visual Analysis of the Medal

**Obverse:** A portrait bust of Jan Hus in profile facing right, wearing a wide-brimmed hat and a draped garment. "IOA" appears to the left and "HVS" to the right of the portrait. Around the rim, the inscription reads: "CREDO VNAM ESSE ECCLESIAM SANC[TAM] CATOLI[CAM]" (I believe in one Holy Catholic Church). The surface shows some oxidation/toning and minor corrosion spots. The relief is somewhat soft, consistent with casting rather than striking.

**The medal's condition** shows age-related surface oxidation and some orange-brown corrosion spots, but the portrait and inscriptions remain clearly legible.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. PRODUCTION DATE IS WRONG: This medal was NOT made in 1415

This is the most significant error in the catalog. Teylers dates the medal to **1415**, the year of Hus's execution. **This is impossible:**

- **The medal as an art form did not exist in 1415.** The earliest known portrait medal is Pisanello's Medal of John VIII Palaeologus, made **c. 1438–1439** — over two decades after 1415. No portrait medals exist from anywhere in Europe before the late 1430s.
- **The "1415" on the medal is the date of Hus's execution**, which is part of the reverse inscription. It is NOT the date of manufacture.
- The medal belongs to the well-documented **"Hustaler" tradition** — commemorative medals depicting Hus that were produced beginning in **c. 1530** during the Protestant Reformation, which claimed Hus as a prophetic predecessor of Luther.

**The earliest known Hustaler** (c. 1530–1537) is attributed to **Hieronymus Magdeburger** (or possibly Hieronymus Dietrich), produced in Jachymov (Joachimsthal), Bohemia. These were made in the context of the Reformation, exactly 100 years after Hus's prophecy.

**This specific medal** most likely dates to **c. 1700–1740**, based on comparison with medals attributed to **Christian Wermuth** (1661–1739) of Gotha, who produced multiple variants of the Hustaler type, especially around the 300th anniversary of Hus's execution (1715/1717). Comparable examples:

| Institution | Material | Diameter | Weight | Attribution |
|-------------|----------|----------|--------|-------------|
| Landesmuseum Württemberg | Silver, cast | 42.2 mm | 16.91 g | Wermuth |
| Berlin Münzkabinett | Silver, struck | 43 mm | 28.88 g | Wermuth, pre-1698 |
| CNG auction | Silver, cast | 41 mm | 21.75 g | Wermuth, c. 1715–1739 |
| **Teylers Museum** | **Silver, cast** | **36.6 mm** | **17.3176 g** | **Unknown (likely Wermuth workshop)** |

The Teylers medal's smaller diameter (36.6 mm vs. standard 41–44 mm) may indicate it is a reduced-format version or an aftercast, which typically produces smaller copies due to metal shrinkage during cooling.

### 2. THE INSCRIPTIONS DECODED

**Obverse:** *"Credo unam esse Ecclesiam Sanctam Catholicam"* — "I believe there is one Holy Catholic Church." This is a paraphrase from the Nicene Creed, placing Hus's commitment to church unity at the center of his memorial.

**Reverse (fully expanded):** *"Centum revolutis annis Deo respondebitis et mihi. Anno a Christo nato 1415 Iohannes Hus condemnatur."*

Translation: **"When a hundred years have passed, you will answer to God and to me. In the year from the birth of Christ 1415, Johannes Hus is condemned."**

This refers to the famous **prophecy attributed to Hus** (though likely apocryphal — it was promoted by Martin Luther in the 1530s): that 100 years after his death, a reformer would arise whom his persecutors could not silence. Luther's 95 Theses in 1517 came almost exactly a century later, and the prophecy was used as powerful Reformation propaganda.

The Teylers medal's abbreviations are heavily compressed compared to the standard Hustaler text, consistent with the smaller diameter requiring more abbreviation.

### 3. "TMNK" PREFIX EXPLAINED

**TMNK = Teylers Museum Numismatisch Kabinet** (Teylers Museum Numismatic Cabinet). The cabinet was formally established with display cases in 1888–1889, though the collection dates to Pieter Teyler's lifetime. The first catalog was written by conservator Theodorus Roest in 1892.

### 4. PIETER TEYLER'S NUMISMATIC COLLECTION

TMNK 00002 is the **second object** in the numismatic collection's numbering — one of the foundation objects. Pieter Teyler van der Hulst (1702–1778), a wealthy Mennonite silk merchant and banker in Haarlem, began collecting around 1750 and amassed over **2,000 coins, medals, and medallions**. The acquisition date of 1778-04-08 is the **date of Teyler's death**, when his entire personal collection passed to the museum foundation. The medal's presence in Teyler's personal collection demonstrates his interest in Reformation history and religious dissent — fitting for a Mennonite, whose own denomination arose from religious reform movements.

### 5. CREATOR ATTRIBUTION SHOULD BE REVISED

Teylers attributes this to "Kingdom of Bohemia (1198-1806)" with an unknown medalist. Given the research, a more accurate attribution would be:

- **Attributed to Christian Wermuth** (1661–1739) **or his workshop**, Gotha, Thuringia
- **After Hieronymus Magdeburger** (fl. c. 1530–1537), who designed the original Hustaler type
- **Kingdom of Bohemia** is the historical context of the subject, not the place of manufacture. The medal was almost certainly made in **Germany** (Gotha or Saxony).

### 6. JAN HUS'S HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE UNDERSTATED

The catalog title "als ketter levend verbrand in 1415" (burned alive as a heretic in 1415) presents only the event. The catalog provides no context for Hus's significance:
- He was a Czech priest, philosopher, and Master at Charles University in Prague
- He criticized the sale of indulgences and advocated church reform, influenced by John Wycliffe
- He was guaranteed safe-conduct by Emperor Sigismund but was arrested, tried, and executed
- His execution triggered the **Hussite Wars** (1419–1436), in which his followers defeated five consecutive papal crusades
- He is considered a forerunner of the Protestant Reformation
- He is a **Czech national hero** — 6 July is a national holiday in the Czech Republic

### 7. CAST, UNEDGED: PRODUCTION CONTEXT

The "cast, unedged" technique confirms this medal was:
- Produced by pouring molten silver into a mold (not struck between dies)
- Left with a plain, untreated edge where the two mold halves met
- Characteristic of workshop production or aftercasting, rather than official mint production
- The softer detail visible in the portrait is consistent with casting or aftercasting
