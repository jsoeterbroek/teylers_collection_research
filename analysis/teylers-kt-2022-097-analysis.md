## Object Description

A large pen drawing (420 x 287 mm) in brown and red ink on paper, attributed to **David Jorisz.** (c. 1501–1556), depicting an allegorical scene in which a figure representing David (identifiable by a poet's wreath, a lyre at his feet, and a sling) battles three chained allegorical figures: Power (holding a globe), Violence (a soldier), and the Body/Flesh (a woman). A skull emphasizes their defeat. Through an arched gateway dated **1536** in the composition, a modern-dressed figure receives scales from heaven — symbolizing spiritual liberation through divine love. The composition is understood as both a biblical illustration and an indirect **self-portrait of David Joris** as the prophetic "third David" following his December 1536 visions. Acquired at Christie's Paris, 18 May 2022 (lot 22, sold for **€126,000**) with support from the Vereniging Rembrandt. Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**. First confirmed drawing by David Joris in any Dutch public collection.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. "JOHANN BALDUNG GRUEN" — TWO FACTUAL ERRORS IN THE INSCRIPTION NOTE (MAJOR)

The catalog records an inscription referencing **"Johann Baldung Gruen (1473-1545)"**. This contains two distinct errors:

**a) First name wrong:** The artist's name is **Hans** Baldung Grien, not "Johann." His father was named Johann Baldung (a jurist), but the artist himself was invariably known as Hans. Every major institution (National Gallery London, Britannica, Metropolitan Museum, Getty, Staatliche Kunsthalle Karlsruhe) lists him as "Hans Baldung Grien."

**b) Birth year wrong:** The catalog (or the inscription as transcribed) gives **1473** as the birth year. All modern scholarly consensus places Baldung's birth at **c. 1484/85** in Schwäbisch Gmünd. The year 1473 is off by approximately 11 years.

If these errors appear in an inscription on the physical drawing (written by a later hand), the catalog should quote the inscription exactly and add a corrective note. Either way, the record as currently presented contains erroneous identification with no corrective apparatus.

### 2. DATE FIELD INTERNALLY CONTRADICTORY (MAJOR)

The production date field gives **"ca. 1536"** while a free-text date field simultaneously states **"ongedateerd; bron Christie's"** (undated; source Christie's). These are contradictory. The year 1536 appears inscribed within the composition (on the arch), recording a moment in David Joris's biography, not necessarily the date of execution. The Vereniging Rembrandt dates the work to **"ca. 1540"** — a further discrepancy. The catalog should clarify that the date "1536" is inscribed within the narrative composition and that the date of execution remains a scholarly question, with estimates ranging from c. 1536 to c. 1540.

### 3. FIRST JORIS DRAWING IN DUTCH PUBLIC COLLECTION — NOT STATED (MAJOR)

The catalog does not note that this is the **first confirmed drawing by David Joris in any Dutch public collection**, as explicitly stated by the Vereniging Rembrandt at the time of acquisition. This is a significant curatorial and art-historical fact.

### 4. BIOGRAPHICAL-THEOLOGICAL CONTEXT ENTIRELY ABSENT (MAJOR)

The drawing's subject is not generic allegory — it reflects a **specific moment in David Joris's biography and theology**. The year 1536 inscribed in the arch refers to the Bocholt Anabaptist congress (August 1536) and Joris's subsequent December 1536 visions, after which he came to see himself as the messianic "third David" — successor to King David and Christ. The figure in the composition is most plausibly a **prophetic self-portrait**. None of this appears in the catalog. The subject terms ("David, ondeugden, allegorie") are wholly inadequate.

### 5. NO LITERATURE CITED (MAJOR)

No bibliography is given. Essential references:
- Gary K. Waite, *David Joris and Dutch Anabaptism, 1524–1543* (Wilfrid Laurier University Press, 1990) — foundational monograph
- Gary K. Waite, "The artist David Joris (1501–1556)" in *The prophet of the renewed senses* (Taylor & Francis, 2021)
- Yvonne Bleyerveld and Ilja M. Veldman, *The Netherlandish Drawings of the 16th Century in Teylers Museum* (Primavera Pers, 2016) — key comparandum
- Christie's Paris sale catalog, 18 May 2022, lot 22

### 6. NO PROVENANCE BEFORE CHRISTIE'S 2022 (MAJOR)

The catalog records the Christie's acquisition but provides no provenance trail before the May 2022 sale. For a 16th-century drawing, the complete absence of any earlier ownership history is a significant gap. The Christie's catalog itself would have recorded prior provenance.

### 7. AUCTION DETAILS INCOMPLETE (MINOR)

The Christie's acquisition is referenced but without key details: Christie's Paris, **18 May 2022, lot 22**; estimate €40,000–60,000; hammer price **€126,000** (including buyer's premium). This is standard provenance information.

### 8. RED INK ICONOGRAPHIC FUNCTION NOT EXPLAINED (MINOR)

The catalog records "pen in bruin, pen in rood" but does not note that the red ink is used **exclusively for flesh tones** in the figures — a technically unusual choice with clear iconographic meaning, foregrounding the carnal/bodily nature of the vices. This is noted in the Vereniging Rembrandt description but absent from Teylers' record.

### 9. OBJECT NAME FIELD MISUSED (MINOR)

The "Object Name" field reads "pen in inkt" — a technique description, not an object type. The field should read "tekening" (drawing).
