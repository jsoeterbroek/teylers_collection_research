## Object Description

A large oil painting on canvas by **Isaac Israëls** (Amsterdam, 1865 – The Hague, 1934), depicting a North African woman in loose-fitting attire beating a drum before a Parisian fairground wrestling tent. Painted during Israëls' Paris period (c. 1904–1913), the painting shows a drummer performing the role of street "barker" to attract spectators to a sideshow. A poster of wrestlers is visible on the wall behind her. The Teylers version (KS 165) is one of at least four known versions of this composition by Israëls; the Rijksmuseum holds a closely related work (SK-C-983). Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. DIMENSIONS DISCREPANCY — POSSIBLE MAJOR ERROR

Multiple independent sources — Wikidata (Q17344479), Wikimedia Commons, and USEUM — consistently record the painting's dimensions as **156.5 x 75 cm**, not 147 x 90 cm as the Teylers catalog states. The discrepancy is substantial: nearly 10 cm difference in height and 15 cm difference in width. One explanation is that 147 x 90 cm refers to the framed dimensions and 156.5 x 75 cm to the canvas, but museum catalogs conventionally record canvas dimensions. This requires **physical verification against the original**.

### 2. DATE RANGE OVERLY BROAD — AND LOWER BOUND INCORRECT

The date range "c. 1903–1914" spans Israëls' entire Paris period. However:

- Israëls did not arrive in Paris until **1904** (not 1903), making 1903 an incorrect lower bound
- Multiple sources (Wikidata, Wikimedia Commons, USEUM) cite the specific year **1908**
- A more defensible range is **c. 1906–1910**, with 1908 being the most frequently cited date

### 3. "CIRCUS MEDRANO" AS SUBJECT — POSSIBLY INCORRECT VENUE

The Cirque Medrano was a **permanent circus building** at 63 Boulevard de Rochechouart. The painting's setting — a drum-beating barker attracting spectators to a **wrestling tent** with a poster of wrestlers visible — is more consistent with a **traveling fairground sideshow** than with the Cirque Medrano proper. Both were in the Montmartre area, but they are distinct venues. The subject tag "Circus Medrano" should be scrutinised.

### 4. MULTIPLE VERSIONS NOT ACKNOWLEDGED

The catalog does not note that this is one of several known versions. At minimum, the following are documented:

| Version | Collection | Dimensions |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| KS 165 | Teylers Museum, Haarlem | 147 x 90 cm (catalog) / 156.5 x 75 cm (external sources) |
| SK-C-983 | Rijksmuseum, Amsterdam | 100 x 65 cm |
| Lot 187 | Sold Sotheby's London, June 2020 | 181 x 75 cm |

Related work: *Worstelaarstent, Parijs* (Wrestling Tent, Paris), Centraal Museum, Utrecht, c. 1910 — the same fairground setting.

### 5. CONTEXT OF THE DRUM: FAIRGROUND BARKER ROLE NOT EXPLAINED

The catalog lists "drum player" as a subject but does not explain the social role. The drummer was a **bonimenteur** — a street performer who beat a drum or made noise to attract audiences to sideshow tents. She is performing labour, not entertainment per se. This context enriches the reading of the painting considerably.

### 6. ARTIST'S SIGNIFICANCE UNDERSTATED

The catalog lists the artist with no context. Isaac Israëls was:

- Son of **Jozef Israëls**, one of the most famous figures of The Hague School
- One of the three central figures of **Amsterdam Impressionism** (alongside Breitner and Witsen)
- A member of the **Tachtigers** movement
- His **auction record**: EUR 625,121 / USD 625,121 for "Ezeltje rijden: a donkey-ride on the beach" (Christie's Amsterdam, 2005)

### 7. TITLE SPELLING — ARCHAIC FORM

"Trommelslaagster" is an older/dialectal Dutch spelling. Modern Dutch uses "Trommelaarster" — the form used by the Rijksmuseum for its version (SK-C-983). While not an error per se, inconsistency between major Dutch museums on the title of related works is worth noting.
