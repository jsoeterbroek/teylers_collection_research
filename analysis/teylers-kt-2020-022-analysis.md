## Object Description

A large watercolor drawing (510 x 680 mm) by **George Hendrik Breitner** (Rotterdam, 1857 – Amsterdam, 1923), depicting the professional model **Adolphe (Louis Edouard) Boutard** (Tebing Tinggi, Sumatra, 1839 – The Hague, 1885) in Orientalist costume. Made c. 1883 in The Hague — likely during a session at Pulchri Studio where Breitner made multiple studies of Boutard under different Orientalist titles ("The Arab," "The Negro," "Seated African"). Acquired by Teylers in 2020. Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. CREDIT LINE NAMES WRONG ORGANIZATION (MAJOR)

The credit line states "VriendenLoterij." The acquisition took place in **2020**. The BankGiro Loterij and VriendenLoterij only merged on **16 August 2021** — after this acquisition. At the time of purchase, the supporting body was the **BankGiro Loterij**, as confirmed by Teylers' own press materials and the BankGiro Loterij website. Retroactively applying the merged name "VriendenLoterij" is factually incorrect. This is the museum's formal institutional acknowledgment of its donor — giving the wrong name is a significant error.

### 2. DATE FIELD INTERNAL CONTRADICTION (MAJOR)

The production date field shows **"ca. 1883"** while a separate date field simultaneously states **"ongedateerd"** (undated). These are contradictory. "Ca. 1883" is a scholarly estimate based on Breitner's documented use of Boutard as a model 1881–1885; "ongedateerd" correctly describes the physical state (no inscribed date). The record should clarify that c. 1883 is a scholarly estimate, not an inscription.

### 3. BOUTARD'S IDENTITY AND CENTURY-LONG MISIDENTIFICATION NOT EXPLAINED (MAJOR)

The catalog lists "Boutard, Adolphe" in the persons keyword field without any contextual note. For over a century, this model was catalogued as **"Adolf Boutar" from Abyssinia (Ethiopia)**. The correct identity has been established through archival research (Svend Veldhuijzen; Frouke van Dijke; Esther Schreuder): **Adolphe Louis Edouard Boutard**, born 27 September 1839 in Tebing Tinggi, Sumatra, to a Belgian KNIL soldier (Accurse Boutard, from Namur) and a Sumatran woman named Siepa, of probable African descent. He died in The Hague, 15 June 1885.

The catalog uses the corrected name but provides no note explaining:
- The history of misidentification (catalogued as "Abyssinian" for over a century)
- His actual Sumatran-African origins
- That "Arabier" is a constructed Orientalist role, not his actual identity

### 4. "ARABIER" TITLE NOT CONTEXTUALISED (MINOR)

The title reflects a 19th-century Orientalist conceit: a non-Arab, non-European model costumed in fantasized Middle Eastern dress for artistic purposes. This is now understood as part of the broader European Orientalist tradition, and modern museum cataloging (Stedelijk Museum, Kunstmuseum Den Haag) includes notes acknowledging this framing. The Teylers catalog does not.

### 5. NO DESCRIPTION WHATSOEVER (MAJOR)

The record has no prose description of what the work shows: how Boutard is posed, what costume he wears, the color palette, or the compositional structure. For a Topstuk, this is a serious deficiency.

### 6. NO PROVENANCE (MAJOR)

No ownership history prior to 2020 is given. The record gives no indication of whether provenance is known or unknown. Works in this Breitner–Boutard series have been actively researched since at least the 2008 "Black is Beautiful: Rubens to Dumas" exhibition (Gemeentemuseum Den Haag).

### 7. NO LITERATURE CITED (MAJOR)

No scholarly references are listed. Directly relevant literature includes:

- Esther Schreuder, "Adolf Boutar," in *Black is Beautiful: Rubens to Dumas* (Gemeentemuseum Den Haag, 2008)
- Esther Schreuder's research blog posts (2014, 2019, 2020) documenting the identity correction
- RKD artist file for Breitner

### 8. RELATED BREITNER–BOUTARD WORKS NOT CROSS-REFERENCED (MAJOR)

The catalog lists two related objects within Teylers' own collection but does not mention the extensive network of Breitner–Boutard works in other Dutch museums:

- **Stedelijk Museum Amsterdam**: oil painting "De Deurwachter/Eunuch/Afrikaanse Strijder" (A 2206, c. 1883)
- **Kröller-Müller Museum**: "Modelstudie van een hurkende man (Adolf Boutar)" (KM 111.153, c. 1885)
- **Museum de Fundatie, Zwolle**: related Boutard watercolor
- **Kunstmuseum Den Haag**: multiple Breitner Boutard drawings

### 9. PULCHRI STUDIO SESSION CONTEXT ABSENT (MINOR)

Research (Schreuder, 2014) established that this watercolor and several other Boutard studies were made in the same session at Pulchri Studio, The Hague — a session that produced works titled "The Arab," "The Negro," and "Seated African," all depicting Boutard. This session context helps explain both the dating and the multiple Orientalist role-play titles.

### 10. OBJECT NAME FIELD MISUSED (MINOR)

The "Object Name" field reads "penseel in waterverf" — a technique description, not a typological name. The field should read "tekening" or "aquarel."
