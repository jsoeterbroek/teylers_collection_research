## Object Description

An oil painting on panel (63 x 81 cm) by **Wybrand Hendriks** (Amsterdam, 1744 – Haarlem, 1831), dated 1799, depicting a session of the **Haarlemse Teekencollegie** — the private drawing society that Hendriks himself founded in 1796 as successor to the dissolved *Haarlemse Stads Teekenacademie* (1772–1795). Members are shown drawing from plaster casts of classical antiquities (Venus de Medici, Venus Callipyge, Apollo Belvedere, and a Vulcan's forge group). Hendriks appears at far right. Six figures are named in the catalog: van Brussel, Visser Bender, Claterbos, Milatz, van der Willigen, Gerlings. Donated to Teylers in 1987 by Mrs. J.H.M. Staring-de Mol van Otterloo. Exhibited in the major 2023 Teylers retrospective *"Wybrand Hendriks was hier!"*. Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. THE PAINTING WAS REWORKED AFTER 1807 — THE "1799" TITLE IS MISLEADING (MAJOR)

The catalog presents this as a documentary view of the Teekencollegie "in 1799." This is demonstrably false for the composition as it currently exists. Scholarly research has established that **at least two figures were added to the panel after 1807**:

- **Adriaan van der Willigen** (1766–1841) was not appointed a director of the Teekencollegie until after 1807 — he was not a member in 1799
- **Jan David Zocher Jr.** (1791–1870), the architect who designed the Vondelpark, was admitted to the Teekencollegie only around 1807/1808, when he was 16 or 17 — eight to nine years after the date inscribed on the painting

The painting is therefore a **composite image reworked after 1807**, not a documentary snapshot of 1799. Presenting it as "Het Teekencollegie van Haarlem **in 1799**" without this qualification is materially misleading.

### 2. JAN DAVID ZOCHER JR. NOT LISTED AS A DEPICTED PERSON (MAJOR)

The catalog names six depicted individuals but **omits Jan David Zocher Jr. (Haarlem, 1791–1870)**, the landscape architect who designed the Vondelpark in Amsterdam. He is documented as depicted in the painting (behind Van der Willigen near the fireplace) and was added post-1807. His omission from the named subjects list is a factual gap.

### 3. HENDRIKS'S ROLE AS TEYLERS CURATOR AND TEEKENCOLLEGIE FOUNDER NOT STATED (MAJOR)

The catalog identifies Hendriks only as "schilder" (painter). He was simultaneously:
- **Kastelein/curator of the Teylers Stichting from 1785–1819** — he literally ran the museum that now owns this painting
- **Founder of the Teekencollegie in 1796** — the very institution he depicted

This dual role — depicting an institution he created, in a museum he curated — is the central art-historical significance of this work. Its absence impoverishes the record completely.

### 4. INSTITUTIONAL HISTORY OF THE TEEKENCOLLEGIE NOT DESCRIBED (MAJOR)

The catalog provides no context for the depicted institution:
- The Teekencollegie was founded in 1796 as direct successor to the *Haarlemse Stads Teekenacademie* (founded 1772, dissolved 1795 upon the death of director Cornelis van Noorde)
- Hendriks renamed it *Kunstmin en Vlijt* in 1809
- The lineage continued in *Kunst zij ons doel* (founded 1821) — still active today as one of the oldest professional artists' associations in the Netherlands

This context is fundamental to understanding the painting's significance as a historical document.

### 5. 2023 EXHIBITION CATALOG NOT CITED (MAJOR)

The 2023 Teylers exhibition *"Wybrand Hendriks was hier!"* (23 September 2023 – 7 January 2024) produced a 248-page scholarly catalog:

> *Wybrand Hendriks (1744-1831): kunstenaar, conservator, kastelein, restaurator, bestuurder, verzamelaar, kunsthandelaar en netwerker* (Waanders, 2023, ISBN 9789462622382)

This is almost certainly the most comprehensive modern treatment of Hendriks's work and is published by the holding institution itself. It is not cited in the catalog record.

### 6. ADRIAAN VAN DER WILLIGEN: NOT DISAMBIGUATED (MINOR)

The catalog lists "van der Willigen" without clarifying that this is **Adriaan van der Willigen (Rotterdam 1766 – Haarlem 1841)**, playwright and art historian, co-author of the foundational *Geschiedenis der Vaderlandsche Schilderkunst* (with Roeland van Eynden) — not to be confused with his nephew **Adriaan van der Willigen Pz. (Hillegom 1810 – Rheden 1876)**, also a prominent Haarlem art historian. And as noted above, this Van der Willigen was added to the painting post-1807.

### 7. NAMED SUBJECTS LACKING BIOGRAPHICAL IDENTIFICATION (MINOR)

The catalog lists six names without biographical context. Two key omissions:

- **Hermanus van Brussel** (Haarlem, 1763 – Utrecht, 1815) — Hendriks's own pupil; landscape painter and etcher; works in the Teylers collection
- **Franciscus Andreas Milatz** (Haarlem, 8 March 1764 – Haarlem, 17 November 1808) — landscape and city painter; became a Teekencollegie member in 1799

### 8. PLASTER CASTS NOT CONTEXTUALISED (MINOR)

The depicted plaster casts (Venus de Medici, Venus Callipyge, Apollo Belvedere, Vulcan's forge group) were transferred from the dissolved Haarlemse Teekenacademie to the new Teekencollegie — a direct material link between the two institutions. Their presence is programmatic, not decorative. The Venus Callipyge in particular is a rare subject in Dutch academic painting of this period.

### 9. NO PROVENANCE BEFORE THE 1987 GIFT (MINOR)

No ownership history before Mrs. Staring-de Mol van Otterloo's 1987 gift is given or noted as unknown.
