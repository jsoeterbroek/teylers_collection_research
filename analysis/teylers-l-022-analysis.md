## Object Description

A pen-and-wash drawing (170 x 237 mm) by **Claude Lorrain** (Claude Gellée, Chamagne, c. 1604/5 – Rome, 1682), dated c. 1640, depicting a solitary draughtsman seen from behind, seated at the mouth of the Grotto of Neptune at Tivoli, sketching the dramatic rock formations around him. One of the most celebrated drawings in the Teylers collection — it served as the **cover image** of the joint Louvre–Teylers Museum exhibition catalog *Claude Gellée, dit Le Lorrain: le dessinateur face à la nature* (2011). Acquired in 1790 as part of the landmark Odescalchi purchase. Designated one of Teylers' **50 Topstukken**.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. GRAY WASH MISSING FROM TECHNIQUE FIELD (MAJOR)

The catalog records the technique as "pen in bruin, penseel in bruin, gewassen." All independent scholarly sources — the Louvre exhibition website, tibursuperbum.it, the 2011 exhibition catalog, and secondary descriptions — consistently describe the technique as **pen and brown ink, brush and brown and gray wash** (recto). The catalog omits **gray wash** (*grijs*) as one of the wash colors. This is a factual incompleteness with consequences for technical art history and conservation records.

### 2. VERSO TECHNIQUE ENTIRELY ABSENT (MAJOR)

The verso is described only iconographically ("kopje en krullende lijn" — head and curling line). The verso has its own documented technique — **graphite, pen and brown ink, and brown wash** — which is entirely absent from the catalog record. A verso with a distinct technique from the recto must be separately documented.

### 3. ROETHLISBERGER CATALOG RAISONNÉ NUMBER NOT CITED (MAJOR)

Marcel Roethlisberger, *Claude Lorrain: The Drawings*, 2 vols. (University of California Press, 1968) is the essential scholarly reference for every Claude Lorrain drawing. Every comparable museum record (British Museum, Getty, Metropolitan Museum) cites the Roethlisberger MRD catalog number. The Teylers record lists bibliography items but does not provide the specific MRD entry number for L 022 in the online record — making the scholarly cross-reference inaccessible to researchers.

### 4. 2011 LOUVRE/TEYLERS EXHIBITION CATALOG NOT CITED (MAJOR)

L 022 was the **cover image** of the joint Louvre–Teylers exhibition:

> Carel van Tuyll van Serooskerken and Michiel C. Plomp, *Claude Gellée, dit Le Lorrain: le dessinateur face à la nature*, Paris: Somogy/Louvre Éditions, 2011.

The exhibition ran at the Louvre (21 April – 18 July 2011) and Teylers Museum (29 November 2011 – 8 January 2012). This is the most authoritative published treatment of this specific drawing. Its absence from the online record is inexplicable.

### 5. PROVENANCE CHAIN INCOMPLETE — QUEEN CHRISTINA OF SWEDEN ABSENT (MAJOR)

The credit line states only "aangekocht van de Odescalchi erfgenamen te Rome, 1790." This omits the distinguished deeper chain:

1. Claude's studio / estate (c. 1640–1682)
2. **Queen Christina of Sweden** (Rome, 1626–1689)
3. Cardinal Decio Azzolino (d. 1689)
4. Marchese Pompeo Azzolino → Principe Livio Odescalchi (c. 1692–1696)
5. Odescalchi heirs
6. Purchased by **Willem Anne Lestevenon** for Teylers, 1790

As with the Michelangelo and Raphael drawings from the same purchase, the Queen Christina provenance is one of the most distinguished collecting chains in Western art history and is entirely absent from the public record.

### 6. DEPICTED SITE NOT GEOGRAPHICALLY IDENTIFIED (MAJOR)

The catalog describes "rotswand met grot" (rocky wall with grotto) without identifying the location. The **Grotto of Neptune** is a real, identifiable site: a large cavern carved by the Aniene River beneath the ancient acropolis of Tivoli (now in Parco Villa Gregoriana, administered by FAI). Scholarly analysis also notes a debate about whether this is the Grotto of Neptune or the adjacent **Grotto of the Sirens** — the identification via comparison with Francis Towne's 1781 annotated drawing "Neptune's Grotto at Tivoli" (Tate T08188) is standard in the literature. Neither the geographic identification nor this debate appears in the catalog.

### 7. DATING METHODOLOGY NOT EXPLAINED (MINOR)

The free-text date field notes "datering van papiertje los in album" (the c. 1640 date derives from a slip of paper found loose in the album). This is an important methodological caveat: the date is not based on documentary evidence from Claude's *Liber Veritatis*, not from Roethlisberger's stylistic analysis, and not from an inscribed date. Dating by a loose paper slip is inherently uncertain. The catalog should flag this explicitly and cross-reference against Roethlisberger's stylistic dating.

### 8. INSCRIPTIONS NOT ANALYZED (MINOR)

The four inscriptions ("Teyler," "Claudio," "Claude Lorrain," "L 22") are listed without interpretation. Standard practice identifies each inscription's source, hand, and approximate date:
- "Teyler" — Teyler Foundation ownership mark
- "Claudio" — old attribution inscription, likely 17th- or early 18th-century Italian hand
- "Claude Lorrain" — later attribution inscription
- "L 22" — Teylers inventory number

### 9. OBJECT NAME FIELD MISUSED (MINOR)

The "Object Name" field reads "pen en penseel in inkt" — a technique description. The field should read "tekening" (drawing).

### 10. "FIGURE DE DOS" CONVENTION NOT CONTEXTUALISED (MINOR)

The seated artist seen from behind (*figure de dos*) is art historically significant: Claude's use of this device is an important antecedent to the Romantic convention famously developed by Caspar David Friedrich. The catalog notes the pose but makes no reference to this art historical resonance.

### 11. TIVOLI BOOK CONNECTION NOT MENTIONED (MINOR)

Claude maintained several bound sketchbooks including a dedicated "Tivoli Book," now broken up and dispersed. L 022 may be traceable to this album; scholars have largely reconstructed its original contents. The catalog makes no reference to this context.
