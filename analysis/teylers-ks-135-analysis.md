## Object Description

An oil painting on panel by **Henriëtte Ronner-Knip** (Amsterdam, 1821 – Brussels, 1909), depicting a cat and four kittens on a piano — one of the signature themes of this enormously successful 19th-century Dutch-Belgian painter. Acquired by Teylers Museum in **2012** with support from the VriendenLoterij, the work is designated one of the museum's **50 Topstukken**. A kitten from the painting, "Brammetje," has become a mascot for Teylers' children's educational programmes.

## Key Findings Not in Teylers' Record

### 1. "ACQUIRED 1897" — LIKELY ERRONEOUS DATE FIELD

Multiple sources indicate the painting was created circa 1890–1897 and **acquired by Teylers in 2012**, not 1897. If the catalog's "acquired" field reads "1897," this conflates the creation date with the acquisition date. The credit line itself contradicts any 1897 acquisition by specifying VriendenLoterij 2012 funding. This is a **major factual error** if the field genuinely states 1897 as an acquisition year.

### 2. ARTIST'S SIGNIFICANCE NOT INDICATED

The catalog lists the artist without context. Ronner-Knip was:

- One of the **most commercially successful female painters in 19th-century Europe**
- In 1887, awarded the **Order of Leopold** by King Leopold II of Belgium — extremely rare for a woman
- In 1901, awarded the **Order of Orange-Nassau** by the Netherlands
- Received royal commissions from Queen Marie Henriette of Belgium, Emperor Wilhelm I, and the Princess of Wales
- **First woman admitted as an "active member"** of the artists' society Arti et Amicitiae in Amsterdam
- Trained exclusively by her father, Josephus Augustus Knip, who knew she would become the family's breadwinner; she sold her first painting at age **15**

### 3. WORKING METHOD NOT DESCRIBED

Ronner-Knip built a specially constructed **glass-fronted studio** in which cats could play freely while she sketched them live. She then modelled them in papier-mâché and used both sketches and sculptures as references. This distinctive method is of significant art-historical interest.

### 4. AUCTION MARKET CONTEXT ABSENT

The catalog provides no indication of the painting's market significance:

- Her auction record: **USD 430,124** for "Kittens at Play" (Sotheby's Amsterdam, 2006)
- A closely comparable work — "The Music Lesson" (oil on panel, 32.5 x 45 cm — nearly identical dimensions) — sold at **Christie's London in July 2018 for GBP 27,500** (approx. EUR 30,800)
- More than **920 works** have appeared at auction historically

### 5. PRE-ACQUISITION PROVENANCE ENTIRELY ABSENT

No ownership history before 2012 is given. For a designated Topstuk, this is a significant omission. The complete provenance chain from creation to Teylers remains undocumented in the public catalog.

### 6. MULTIPLE VERSIONS NOT NOTED

Ronner-Knip painted the piano/music-with-cats theme multiple times. The Teylers painting is one of at least three known versions:

- **"The Piano Lesson"** (oil on canvas, 44.4 x 66.7 cm, c. 1885) — a larger variant
- **"The Music Lesson"** (oil on panel, 32.5 x 45 cm) — nearly identical format, sold Christie's 2018
- **KS 135** (oil on panel, 33 x 44 cm, c. 1890–1897) — the Teylers version

### 7. STANDARD REFERENCES NOT CITED

The catalog cites no literature. For a Topstuk, the absence of the principal monograph is notable:

- **Han van der Horst**, *Cats and Kittens at Play: The Art of Henriette Ronner-Knip 1821–1909* (Lund Humphries, ISBN 9781851492824) — the only published monograph on the artist

### 8. "BRAMMETJE" EDUCATIONAL ROLE NOT MENTIONED

A kitten visible in the painting has been named "Brammetje" and adopted as the mascot for Teylers' family programming, including an online picture book and children's tour. This public role is not reflected in the catalog record.
